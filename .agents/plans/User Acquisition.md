# MindFlow Waitlist and Prospecting Plan

## Summary

Build a small, qualified waitlist for English-speaking Indian professionals aged 22–35 who experience blank-page journaling friction. Until privacy and product-readiness checks pass, collect interest only; afterward, invite the strongest prospects to the ₹299 founding beta.

The immediate target is:

- 30 qualified prospects
- 15 genuine conversations
- 10 qualified waitlist signups
- First 5 paid founding users
- Expansion to 20 only after activation and Day-2 retention are validated

## Acquisition Strategy

- Make the founder’s LinkedIn profile the primary channel, Instagram the repurposing channel, and WhatsApp/DM outreach the main conversion channel.
- Use Reddit and professional communities for helpful participation, not direct promotion. Reddit communities may restrict promotional content and commonly expect roughly 90% non-promotional participation. [Reddit guidance](https://support.reddithelp.com/hc/en-us/articles/28012014962580-How-do-I-keep-spam-out-of-my-community)
- Deprioritize X, YouTube, paid advertising, and a separate brand account until the first five customers reveal which message converts.
- Use one consistent CTA: “I’m inviting a small group to try a seven-day guided journaling experience. Join the interest list.”
- Waitlist form fields: first name, email, age-18+ confirmation, current journaling frequency, biggest journaling difficulty, and optional willingness to pay ₹299. Do not request medical or mental-health information.
- After signup, send a personal acknowledgement and ask one non-sensitive question: “What usually stops you from journaling consistently?”

## Social Content System

Publish three founder-led pieces each week:

- Monday: practical advice for overcoming blank-page friction.
- Wednesday: transparent build lesson, product decision, or privacy principle.
- Friday: one usable journaling prompt followed by the waitlist CTA.
- Daily: spend 15–20 minutes leaving genuinely useful comments on posts about productivity, reflection, creator/founder life, and journaling.

Repurpose each idea into:

- LinkedIn: short story or observation, practical takeaway, one question, CTA.
- Instagram: carousel or founder-to-camera Reel using the same idea.
- WhatsApp Status: one-sentence insight, screenshot, or prompt with the waitlist link.
- Reddit/community posts: standalone useful answer with no link unless rules and context permit it.

LinkedIn should remain personal and useful rather than promotional; its own guidance emphasizes clear themes, consistent publishing, questions, and educational value. [LinkedIn newsletter guidance](https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a517940/linkedin-newsletters-best-practices?lang=en)

Content pillars:

1. Why people abandon journaling.
2. One-minute reflection exercises.
3. Overthinking versus writing something concrete.
4. Building MindFlow and deliberately removing unnecessary features.
5. Privacy, AI consent, and what MindFlow does not do.
6. Real objections and lessons, anonymized without journal content.

Avoid claims about treating anxiety, improving mental health, diagnosis, therapy, or guaranteed emotional outcomes.

## Outreach and Measurement

- Send five personalized messages on Day 1 and five on Day 2.
- Mention the specific reason each person came to mind.
- Ask permission to send details instead of dropping a link immediately.
- Follow up once after 48–72 hours. Send a second follow-up only after prior interest.
- Ask interested contacts for one relevant introduction.
- Approach community managers with a free 20-minute guided-reflection session, not a mass promotional-post request.
- Review results weekly by raw counts: messages, replies, conversations, waitlist signups, qualified signups, paid conversions, first-entry activation, and Day-2 returns.
- Rewrite the hook if fewer than 3 of the first 10 personalized messages produce conversations.
- Fix the landing page or trust messaging if conversations occur but signups do not.
- Do not increase outreach volume while privacy, payment access, activation, or retention is failing.

## Acceptance Criteria and Assumptions

- A functioning waitlist page and confirmation message exist before publishing.
- The first campaign includes a 30-prospect tracker and a three-week content calendar.
- Every post uses one measurable waitlist link.
- No payments are accepted until cross-user privacy, payment-to-access, and first-reflection checks pass.
- Default audience is Indian young professionals; messaging is English-first and non-clinical.
- Public organizations are partnership prospects. Named individual users must come from the founder’s own network or consensual referrals rather than scraping people based on sensitive traits.
