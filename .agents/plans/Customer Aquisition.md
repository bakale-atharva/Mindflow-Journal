# MindFlow Journal — Founding Beta Customer Acquisition Plan

Status: Canonical  
Last updated: 2026-07-13

## Objective

Acquire five paid founding users, repair the complete journey, then expand toward twenty. The previous 1,000-customer target is a long-term ambition, not the current execution target.

## Offer

- 7-Day Emotional Reset for adults aged 18+.
- Seven focused daily prompts, private entries, optional mood check-ins, and one concise AI reflection when the user consents to Groq processing.
- ₹299 one-time with a seven-day refund window.
- Limited to 20 founding users.
- Access delivered within 12 hours after captured-payment verification.
- No lifetime Pro discount is advertised until pricing and unit economics are defined.

## Readiness Gate

Do not send a purchase link until:

- Carrd and a Razorpay test payment work.
- Allowlisted magic-link login works.
- Cross-user data isolation is verified.
- Day 1 entry and reflection work on mobile and desktop.
- The no-AI-consent journaling path works.
- Privacy, refund, AI limitation, 18+, and non-clinical copy are published.
- The payment and access runbook has been rehearsed.

## Funnel

Qualified conversation → Carrd → captured Razorpay payment → access within 12 hours → first login → Day 1 entry → reflection viewed when consented → Day-2 return → seven entries within ten days → interview.

## Channel Priority

1. **Warm outreach:** 5–10 thoughtful messages per day to people with a specific problem-fit reason.
2. **Founder content:** three useful posts per week about overthinking, reflection, and blank-page friction.
3. **Community participation:** answer relevant questions first; mention MindFlow only when useful and permitted.
4. **Follow-up:** once after 48–72 hours; a second follow-up only after explicit interest.

Manual posting is sufficient. Use Typefully only when inconsistency becomes a demonstrated problem.

## First-Five Sequence

1. Build a prospect list of 30 and select the 10 strongest warm fits.
2. Send five personalized messages on Day 1 and five on Day 2.
3. Record replies and exact objections.
4. Send interested people to Carrd rather than explaining an expanding feature list in DMs.
5. Verify each payment, grant access, and confirm login.
6. Check activation after 24 hours and offer technical help only.
7. Interview every user after completion or abandonment.
8. Fix only purchase, access, privacy, activation, reflection, return, and completion failures.

## Customer Tracker

Record name, email, source, problem-fit reason, contact dates, reply, objection, payment reference, captured time, access time, first login, first entry, reflection viewed, Day-2 return, completion, refund, interview outcome, issue, and next action.

Never copy journal content into the tracker.

## Initial Gates

- Five captured payments.
- At least four of five activate within 24 hours of access.
- At least three of five return on Day 2.
- Zero cross-user exposure.
- Payment-to-access time stays within 12 hours.
- Every initial user produces an interview outcome, including abandoned users.

Use raw counts for the first five rather than percentages.

## Interview Guide

1. What was happening when you decided to try MindFlow?
2. What did you expect the seven days to help you do?
3. Where did you hesitate during payment, access, or the first entry?
4. What felt useful, generic, uncomfortable, or unnecessary about the reflection?
5. What made you return or stop returning?
6. What would make the product worth continuing to use or pay for?

Do not ask leading questions that assume the product created clarity.

## Content Cadence

- Monday: a practical idea for reducing blank-page friction.
- Wednesday: a specific founder/build lesson.
- Friday: one journaling prompt and a clear founding-beta invitation.
- Daily: 15–20 minutes of relevant, useful replies.

## Stop Conditions

- Pause immediately for unauthorized access or private-data exposure.
- Pause payments if access repeatedly exceeds 12 hours.
- Rewrite positioning if qualified users interpret MindFlow as therapy.
- Investigate onboarding if fewer than four of five activate.
- Investigate core value if users activate but do not return.
- Do not increase outreach volume until the failing step is repaired.
